Indian Life Sciences — Documentary Series Overview

Suprabha Trust  ·  Indian Life Sciences Documentary Series  ·  Hyderabad

This documentary on "Indian Life Sciences" unveils the subtle scientific aspects of Indian life sciences which are unknown and difficult to understand. Subjects are analyzed from four perspectives — Scientific, Spiritual, Historical, and Natural — to achieve a holistic view. "This project answers many questions related to Indian life sciences: whether it is a myth or real science."

Introduction & Scope

Indian Life Sciences form part of the daily routine in India. This project unveils subtle scientific aspects of Indian life sciences which are unknown and difficult to understand. The subjects covered are:

  • Vedic Chanting · Astrology · Kamasutra (Sexology)
  • Yoga (exercise for body, mind and soul)
  • Ayurveda (medicinal system) · Vastu Shastra (engineering)
  • Kala Shastra (Music & Dance) · Indian Festivals
  • Temples (energized prayer houses)
  • Agori System & Black Magic · Transformation of Spirituality
  • Puja (different forms of prayers)

Key Objective

The primary focus is the development and amalgamation of science with spirituality dating thousands of years back. Many documents have been made on this subject in small segments — but none have had a holistic approach. This project answers key questions:

  • Was God there before religious systems were formed?
  • Is there an intelligence energy form in the cosmos?
  • Is there any system to grasp cosmic energy?
  • Can human beings rejuvenate their forces — and what are the future prospects?

1. Yoga

Yoga is a shaping system of the human body through physical exercise. Yoga not only shapes the human body but removes any blockage in the physiological functioning of the body. There are different types of yoga systems practiced worldwide for rejuvenation.

  • Developed to overcome the five elements on which human beings depend
  • Increases the tolerance capacity in humans through a methodical way
  • Helpful to redefine human endurance to all critical circumstances
  • Connects physical health with spiritual evolution

2. Astrology

Indian astrology is a very advanced technique primarily dealing with cosmic light. The Vedic mathematics is an advanced form of calculation used in Indian astrology. The most important aspect is cosmic light falling on the human body — intensity of light and colour keep changing in different timeframes, making the human body act and behave differently.

  • Functions like a scanner identifying biological and physiological defects
  • Has remedies by balancing light intensities through different practices
  • The mind captures one's own actions and registers in DNA — passed to next generations (hereditary)
  • Calculation of light years took thousands of years of systematic observation

3. Temples of India

Temples are energy centers receiving pure cosmic energy. A devotee who prays receives this energy and gets a prosperous life. This construction format is one of the oldest in the world.

  • Vibrations made in a temple complex help attain spiritual well-being to the devotee
  • Old temples were protected from negative elements hidden in air, vibrations, light, and magnetic fields
  • A devotee fully dedicating to the services of God receives positive energy from the Temples
  • Temple architecture (Vastu-based) designed to condense and channel cosmic energy

4. Traditional Worshipping — Puja

Worshipping God in Hinduism is called Puja. Various forms of puja systems are adopted across the country — the single common thread is that "God is within the human body."

  • The main element of puja is sacrifice to nature — which gives an abundant supply of basic requirements
  • A system to clean up body, mind, and soul to attain a clear conscience
  • Contains elements of physiology, medicine, and biological systems for healthy and prosperous life
  • Over time it has lost its actual essence — Suprabha Trust works to restore understanding

5. Ayurveda

Ayurveda is a medical system that works naturally without side effects. Most advanced treatment therapies are lost over time due to natural calamities.

  • Touches bio-technology and other scientific aspects in ways modern medicine is only now discovering
  • Surgery as part of ancient healing system — dating thousands of years back
  • Physical, super-physical, and meta-physical healing all integrated
  • Vibrational medicine through mantras, herbs, metals, and oils

6. Spirituality in India

Spirituality in India is a way of life registered in history. Many religions in India co-exist from a very long time, each with different perspectives reaching the ultimate — all strings attached are the same but in different forms.

  • Bhakti movement spans: Bhagavad Gita, Lord Krishna, Kabir, Gurunanak, Buddha, Muslim King Tanisha, Ramdas and saints from different periods and religions
  • All realized that without completely surrendering to the ultimate, one cannot reach the almighty
  • Maha Kumbh — taking a holy dip in a river at a particular time, worshiping nature — has scientific theory attached

7. Agori System

The Agori system in India holds the belief that all human beings of the universe are from one family. Building up of energies and their effect on the human body is purely scientific but in a subtle form. The way a human thinks about certain aspects of life enables growth in the same line of thinking.

8. Hinduism

Hinduism is a way of life which makes a happy society and a healthy environment. Formed and redefined on the basis of ancient Vedic tradition, it has taken care of many aspects of human biological function, nature, and spiritual system. The actual know-how of Vedic sciences was lost by the time of Indus Valley Civilization — and only a few remaining elements were absorbed.

9. Dance & Music

Sound, Dance, and Music took shape during the Vedic period — with time the whole system got redefined into cultural arts. Sound, Dance, and Music are based on human physiology and nature.

  • Music and Dance in proper order nurtures Body, Mind, and Soul
  • Has become an integral part of lifestyle and cultural identity
  • Many sounds used in daily life carry significant scientific meaning
  • Ragas align with time, season, and cosmic cycles — a complete frequency science

10. Vastu Shastra

Vaastu is called Vedic engineering — linked with five elements: Air, Water, Sky, Fire, and Earth. The magnetic poles of Earth and the planetary system are connected to Vastu Shastra. Indian temples are constructed on the basis of Vaastu and have thousands of years of heritage.

  • Deals with the placement of natural elements for synergy in living
  • Significant for its technique to withhold the pressures of nature
  • Aligns physical spaces with planetary magnetic forces
  • Creates energy-efficient, harmonious living environments

11. Kamasutra

Kamasutra is the sexology system in India with many sections. The most important aspect links with biological and physiological functions of the body — demarking postures and gestures that support or hinder well-being. In a spiritual perspective it relaxes the body, mind, and soul through physiological contentment and provides bliss to human beings.

12. Vedic Sciences

The Vedic sciences are the roots of the life sciences — Rig-Veda being the first among the Vedic system. Vedas have only sound value, not conventional meaning.

  • The vibration of Vedic chanting nurtures the human body and attracts positive energy
  • Vedic chants help in influencing the environment — beneficial not only for humans but all natural forms
  • Vedic system is linked with logic and mathematics — a very advanced scientific system
  • Helps in the union of human with universal consciousness (God)

13. Traditions & Festivals of India

Traditions, culture, and festivals in India carry Scientific, Social, and Philosophical perspectives.

  • Using cow dung in front of the house — kills germs (antimicrobial properties)
  • Watering the Tulsi plant — its ether kills germs and purifies air
  • Colours and lights used in festivals affect physiological functioning of the body
  • The main fundamental of Hinduism: to have a happy society
  • Festivals are sacrifice to nature — in return nature provides abundant supplies to living forms

Conclusion

These life sciences are interconnected and connected by the five elements — Air, Water, Earth, Fire, and Sky — with which humans have a constant interaction. A system was introduced in the form of life sciences which balances them both for a healthy and prosperous living. Human beings also discovered intelligence energy forms in the universe.

  • All 13 life sciences are interconnected through the Pancha Bhuthas (five elements)
  • The perspective is to show a path to survive with harsh natural elements
  • To make the human body prepared for all possible disorders through these sciences
  • Five elements are helpful and destructive — the life sciences balance them for prosperity
  • Intelligence energy forms discovered in the universe — connecting human to cosmic consciousness
