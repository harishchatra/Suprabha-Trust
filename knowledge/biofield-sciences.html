The Human Body — Beyond the Physical

Modern science has extensively mapped the physical body through its Lymphatic, Nervous, Cardiovascular, and Visceral systems. However, ancient Vedic knowledge recognises that the human being extends far beyond these visible systems — into the realm of Human Energy Fields and the Kosha system of subtle sheaths.

The Kosha system in Vedic tradition describes five sheaths of existence surrounding the Atman (soul): the physical body (Annamaya), the vital energy body (Pranamaya), the mental body (Manomaya), the wisdom body (Vijnanamaya), and the bliss body (Anandamaya). Bio-field science provides the modern instrumentation to observe and measure these ancient Vedic insights.

Kirlian Photography — Capturing the Bio-field

Kirlian photography is a technique that captures the electro-photonic emanations (aura/bio-field) of living and non-living objects on photographic film. It provides visible evidence of the energy fields that surround all matter.

  • Phantom Leaf Experiment — Kirlian images of a leaf with a portion physically removed still show the complete leaf's energy field, demonstrating that the bio-field template precedes and outlasts physical form
  • Fresh vs Stored Food — Kirlian images clearly show the superior energy field of fresh food compared to stored food — direct evidence of life force depletion over time
  • White vs Wholemeal Bread — Kirlian imaging shows measurably higher bio-field energy in wholemeal bread versus refined white bread
  • Garlic & Pepper — Each herb and food item shows a unique, characteristic energy signature in Kirlian photography

Microwaves Deplete Energy Levels

One of the most striking findings in bio-field research: Kirlian images of microwaved food versus fresh food show a dramatic depletion of the bio-field energy. A fresh apple photographed in Kirlian shows a rich, expansive energy field — the same apple after microwaving shows a drastically reduced or absent energy signature.

This provides scientific validation for the ancient Vedic principle that food is not merely physical nourishment but also pranic (life force) nourishment — and that the method of preparation profoundly affects its healing and sustaining capacity.

Each Entity Has a Unique Dynamic Energy Field

Bio-field science demonstrates that every entity — living or non-living — carries its own unique and dynamic energy field. Research using PIP (Polycontrast Interference Photography) technology has documented the energy fields of:

  • Rudraksha beads — showing highly elevated, structured energy patterns recognised in Vedic tradition as spiritually potent
  • Gemstones (Emeralds) — unique crystalline energy signatures measurably different from ordinary stones
  • Plants and herbs — distinct bio-fields that vary with health, freshness, and species
  • Human beings — complex, layered energy fields that change with thought, emotion, health, and spiritual practice

Food Energies — PIP Research

PIP imaging of different foods reveals their relative pranic energy content. Research findings include:

  • Palak (Spinach) — high bio-field energy; rich prana content
  • Green Chilli — intense, concentrated energy signature
  • Wheat (Gahu) — balanced, stable energy field
  • Nuts — high-density energy content; strong bio-field
  • Supari + Tobacco — disrupted, chaotic energy signature — consistent with harmful physiological effects

These findings validate the ancient Ayurvedic classification of foods as Sattvic (pure, life-giving), Rajasic (stimulating), and Tamasic (depleting) — now measurably demonstrable through bio-field imaging.

Thoughts Reflect in the Energy Field / Aura

This inherent energy of the aura indicates soul maturity and energy levels. Bio-field research conclusively shows that mental and emotional states are reflected immediately in the aura:

  • Stressed with positive hyperactivity — expanded but chaotic, jagged aura patterns
  • Stressed and depressed person — contracted, dark, depleted aura with significant energy gaps
  • Anger — sharp, red, disrupted energy spikes around the aura
  • Love and Compassion — warm, expansive, coherent green and pink aura patterns
  • Depression — collapsed, dark, inward-pulling energy field

Change in thought patterns directly changes the AURA — providing a measurable basis for the Vedic teaching that thought is the most powerful force in shaping human reality and health.

  • Stressed energy fields restored to healthy patterns after consistent meditation practice
  • Higher consciousness states visually reflected as larger, more luminous auras
