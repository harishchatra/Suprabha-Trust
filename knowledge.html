  • The human body mirrors Earth geometry — its proportions, cycles, and frequencies echo planetary patterns
  • What affects the Earth from the cosmos has direct implications on the human body
  • Human physiological rhythms (breathing, heartbeat, sleep cycles) correspond to cosmic and planetary cycles
  • The 33 trillion cells of the human body mirror the organisation of cosmic intelligence
  • The five elements (Pancha Bhuthas) in the human body mirror the five elements of the natural world

Human Being — A Bundle of Evolving Energies

In Sanatana Dharma, the human being is understood as a dynamic bundle of energies in a constant state of evolution and change:

  • These energies continuously change due to activities — physical, mental, and spiritual
  • Every thought, action, and interaction creates a measurable energy shift in the human bio-field
  • The direction of this evolution — toward higher consciousness or away from it — depends on the choices made
  • Dharma provides the map for navigating this energy evolution toward its highest potential

Why Does the Human System Need Dharma?

All other beings in nature operate on Nature's Dynamic — they follow the natural law automatically, instinctively, and without deviation. The plant grows toward light, the river flows to the sea, the bird migrates at the right season. They have no choice — and therefore no karma.

Human beings are unique: they live and work on their own intellect and conscious choice. This freedom — the greatest gift — also carries the greatest responsibility. Without Dharma as a guiding framework, human intellect can work against nature, against society, and against the individual's own highest good.

  • Human beings with the aura change with different activities — each choice alters the energy field
  • The Law of Karma applies: every action generates a corresponding energy consequence
  • Dharma provides the framework for making choices that align with Universal Law
  • Without Dharma, the human being drifts from natural balance into disorder, disease, and destruction

The Aura of Human Diminishes with Untruth

Sanatana Dharma recognises what modern bio-field science has now confirmed: the human aura — the measurable energy field surrounding the body — responds directly to the quality of life lived:

  • Dharmic life / Truthful living — the aura expands, brightens, and becomes more coherent; health, prosperity, and clarity follow
  • Adharmic life / Untruthful living — the aura diminishes, darkens, and becomes chaotic; disease, conflict, and destruction follow
  • The truth diminishes with an irregular and unpatterned way of life
  • With Dharmic life the truth prevails in society — collective coherence is maintained

The Cascading Effect of Untruth in Society

Sanatana Dharma teaches that individual Adharma creates ripples that extend far beyond the individual:

  • When untruth prevails, it becomes destructive to society — not through punishment but through the natural consequence of energy depletion
  • Whoever seeds untruth in the society will create destruction — a direct energetic law, not merely a moral judgment
  • The diminished energy levels of individuals create a cascading effect of collective destruction
  • Untruth destroys the pure energy of the human being and society at large

Sanatana Dharma Improves Self and Society

The transformative power of Sanatana Dharma operates at both the individual and collective level simultaneously:

  • Sanatana Dharma helps improve not only one's self but total society — a healthy individual creates a healthy community
  • By adopting Sanatana Dharma as a way of life, healthy energy improves and karmic systems start evolving positively
  • This results in a healthy society — one where truth, cooperation, and natural law prevail
  • PIP bio-field research visually confirms: the healthy form on the left shows expanded, coherent energy; the weakened form on the right shows depleted, incoherent energy

Dharma, Karma & Moksha — The Complete Path

Sanatana Dharma provides the complete framework for human evolution:

  • Dharma — living in alignment with Universal Law; righteous conduct, thought, and dealings
  • Karma — the law of energetic consequence; every action generates a corresponding energy return
  • Moksha — liberation through the Cosmic Constitution; the ultimate goal of human life — freedom from the cycle of birth and death through truthful, Dharmic living

Human beings need to live a balanced life to attain Moksha / Salvation through the Cosmic Constitution. A truthful life — aligned with Achar, Vichar, and Vyavahar — is the direct path to this ultimate liberation. Sanatana Dharma is not a restriction but a map to the highest freedom.
