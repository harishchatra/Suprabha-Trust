Sanatana Dharma

Three Aspects of Practice

Suprabha Trust explores Sanatana Dharma through three fundamental dimensions that span the cosmic, divine, and spiritual — each offering a unique lens into ancient Indian knowledge systems observed for over 75,000 years.

Nataraj — Adhi Bhauthika

Rigveda — Adhi Daivika

Chakras — Adhyatmika

Adhi Bhauthika

The Physical Plane

The tangible world of matter, body, and natural forces. Encompasses Vaasthu, Ayurveda, agriculture, ecology, and the physical sciences that govern life on Earth and the cosmos.

Adhi Daivika

The Divine Plane

The realm of cosmic intelligence — the Vedas, Upanishads, Puranas, and the laws governing the planetary, stellar, and universal systems observed by ancient Rishis and Munis.

Adhyatmika

The Spiritual Plane

The inner dimension of self — Yoga, Meditation, Pranayama, the Chakra system, and the journey from individual consciousness (Atman) to universal consciousness (Brahman).

Nataraj at CERN

Science meets Vedic Wisdom

Sacred Spaces

Temple Architecture

Documenting the sacred geometry, iconography, and ritual sciences embedded in India's ancient temple architecture — from the Dravidian Gopurams to the North Indian Shikhara style.

Heritage Sites

Living Heritage

Studying the material culture of India's great empires — the Vijayanagara at Hampi, the Chola bronzes, Ajanta–Ellora murals, and the traditions that survived centuries of transformation.

Ancient Texts

Manuscript Preservation

Locating, cataloguing, and digitising rare manuscripts — palm-leaf scriptures, Sanskrit shlokas, and the oral traditions of Rishis who encoded universal truths in Beejakshara syllables.

Global Dialogue

Science & Dharma

Fostering dialogue between Vedic knowledge and modern science — as demonstrated by the placement of the Nataraj at CERN, a symbol of Shiva's cosmic dance and the dance of particles.

Our Purpose

Mission, Vision & Values

Mission

To discover, document, and disseminate India's mystic sciences, ancient wisdom, and Vedic traditions for the benefit of present and future generations.

Vision

A world that recognises the depth, diversity, and scientific rigour of Sanatana Dharma — a civilisation where humanity's inner and outer knowledge are unified.
