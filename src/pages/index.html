Understanding Sanatana Dharma

Three Aspects of Practice

We explore Sanatana Dharma through three fundamental dimensions that span the cosmic, divine, and spiritual — each offering a unique lens into ancient Indian knowledge systems.

Primary Focus

Adhi Bhauthika

A rare and thoroughly scientific practice relating not only to the world but also to cosmic sciences. This aspect makes our work unique — connecting earthly phenomena with universal principles.

The Divine Aspect

Adhi Daivika

The divine dimension of Sanatana Dharma practices, connecting spiritual wisdom with cosmic consciousness and universal energies. A bridge between human experience and the greater forces that govern existence.

The Spiritual Dimension

Adhyatmika

The innermost spiritual dimension of ancient practices, focusing on inner consciousness and the journey toward self-realization — the ultimate purpose of all Sanatana Dharma traditions.

Who We Are

Mission & Vision

To conserve, document, and celebrate India's traditional and cultural sciences through systematic research, heritage conservation, educational programs, and community partnerships.

We envision a world where India's traditional and cultural sciences are recognized, valued, and integrated into contemporary life.

Our discourses are shaped through discussions with Historians, Spiritual Gurus, Resource Persons, Scientists, and Cultus — bringing together diverse perspectives.
