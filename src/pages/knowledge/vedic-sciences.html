The Four Vedas

The Vedas are sound values that possess multidimensional wisdom in each Beejakshara. These Beejaksharas represent ultimate truth encoded with energy and mathematics. The ancients observed immense knowledge and wisdom in the Universe and beyond, described extensively in different Vedas by various sages. Divided into four by Veda Vyaasa (Vyaasa Maharshi), the author of the Mahabharata:

  • Rig Veda — hymns and cosmic knowledge
  • Yajur Veda — ritual formulas and procedures
  • Sama Veda — musical chants and melodies
  • Atharvana Veda — healing, protection, and daily life

The Six Darshanas

  • Sankhya — dualistic philosophy of consciousness (Puruṣa) and matter (Prakṛti)
  • Yoga — integrates Sankhya with practical techniques for controlling the mind and spiritual liberation
  • Nyaya & Vaisheshika — Logic and Atomism; understanding reality through valid knowledge and atomic theory
  • Mimamsa — interpreting Vedic rituals connected to karma and dharma; posits an eternal universe governed by action and consequences
  • Vedanta — emphasizes the Upanishads, Brahma Sutra, and Bhagavad Gita; key interpretations: Dvaita, Advaita, Visishtadvaita

Pancha Purusharthas — Four Goals of Life

  • Dharma — righteous conduct and duty
  • Artha — material prosperity and security
  • Kama — pleasure, love, and desire
  • Moksha — liberation through self-realisation

Six Vedangas — Auxiliary Sciences

  • Shiksha — Phonetics: Sanskrit alphabet, accent, melody, euphonic combination
  • Chanda — Poetic meters and vibrational rhythm of Vedic verse
  • Vyakarana — Grammar and linguistic analysis for precise expression
  • Nirukta — Etymology; explanation of ancient words and their proper meanings
  • Kalpa — Ritual science; rites of passage from birth, weddings to death
  • Jyotishya — Vedic timekeeping through nakshatra and astronomical observations
