What is Sanatana Dharma?

Sanatana Dharma is not a religion in the conventional sense — it is the eternal, universal operating system of existence. Its four dimensions are:

  • Ultimate Truth — the absolute, unchanging reality underlying all phenomena
  • Universal Constitution — the cosmic law that governs all existence from subatomic to galactic scales
  • Universal Application — principles that apply equally to all life forms, times, and places
  • Ecological Application — the harmonisation of human activity with natural and cosmic forces
  • Human Being Standardised Applications — codified practices for aligning human behaviour with Universal Law
  • Behavioural Patterns / Applications — the Dharmic way of life expressed through Achar (conduct), Vichar (thought), and Vyavahar (behaviour)

Why Does the Human System Need Dharma?

All other beings in nature operate on Nature's Dynamic — they follow the natural law automatically, instinctively, and without deviation. The plant grows toward light, the river flows to the sea, the bird migrates at the right season. They have no choice — and therefore no karma.

Human beings are unique: they live and work on their own intellect and conscious choice. This freedom — the greatest gift — also carries the greatest responsibility. Without Dharma as a guiding framework, human intellect can work against nature, against society, and against the individual's own highest good.

  • Human beings with the aura change with different activities — each choice alters the energy field
  • The Law of Karma applies: every action generates a corresponding energy consequence
  • Dharma provides the framework for making choices that align with Universal Law
  • Without Dharma, the human being drifts from natural balance into disorder, disease, and destruction
